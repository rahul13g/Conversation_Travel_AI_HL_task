# Destination: Northeast India (Meghalaya + Sikkim + Assam + Arunachal Pradesh)

## Overview
Northeast India is India's most underrated and extraordinary travel region — living root bridges, the world's wettest place, Buddhist monasteries above clouds, one-horned rhinos, and the cleanest rivers in India. An emerging destination for travelers seeking something completely different from mainstream India tourism.

## Best For
- Adventure and nature seekers
- Off-beat travelers
- Trekkers
- Wildlife enthusiasts
- Photography lovers
- Solo travelers and couples

## Travel Duration
- Short: 5 Nights / 6 Days (single state)
- Standard: 7 Nights / 8 Days (2 states)
- Extended: 10 Nights / 11 Days (multi-state circuit)

## Getting There
- Flights to Guwahati (main hub), Bagdogra (for Sikkim), Shillong, Dimapur
- Train to Guwahati from most major cities
- Road: Well connected within Northeast by state highways + NH

## Best Time to Visit
- Ideal: October to April
- Monsoon (June–September): Meghalaya is lush and spectacular but travel is difficult
- Winter (December–February): Cold but clear — best for Sikkim and Arunachal
- Avoid: Heavy monsoon months for remote areas

## State Highlights

### Meghalaya — The Abode of Clouds
- Double Decker Living Root Bridge — Nongriat village trek (3,500 steps)
- Dawki River — clearest river in India, boat rides with visible riverbed
- Mawlynnong — Asia's cleanest village
- Cherrapunji (Sohra) — world's wettest place, waterfalls, caves
- Mawsmai Cave — limestone cave
- Umiam Lake — boating
- Shillong — Scotland of the East, cafes, music scene

### Sikkim — The Mountain Kingdom
- Gangtok — the charming capital
- Tsomgo (Changu) Lake — glacial lake at 3,753m
- Nathula Pass — India-China border (permit required)
- Rumtek Monastery — one of Buddhism's most sacred sites
- Pelling — Kanchenjunga panoramic views
- Yuksom — gateway to Goecha La trek
- Zero Point — snow point near Lachung

### Assam — Wildlife and Culture
- Kaziranga National Park — UNESCO, largest one-horned rhino population
- Majuli Island — world's largest river island, Vaishnavite monasteries
- Kamakhya Temple — one of India's most important Shakti temples
- Brahmaputra River cruise
- Jorhat — tea garden tours and tasting

### Arunachal Pradesh
- Tawang Monastery — largest monastery in India, 400-year-old
- Sela Pass — snow pass at 4,170m
- Ziro Valley — UNESCO nominated, Apatani tribe culture
- Namdapha National Park — Snow Leopards, tigers, red pandas

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Northeast mein permit lagta hai?
A: Arunachal Pradesh ke liye Inner Line Permit (ILP) chahiye Indian nationals ko — hum arrange karte hain. Nathula Pass (Sikkim) ke liye bhi permit hai, included in package.

Q: Living root bridges trek kitna tough hai?
A: Double Decker ke liye 3,500+ steps down and up — moderate to tough. Physically fit travelers ke liye. Single root bridge options bhi available hain (easier).

Q: Kaziranga mein rhino guaranteed dikhega?
A: Kaziranga mein rhino sighting almost guaranteed hai — density world's highest hai. Tiger aur elephant bhi frequently spotted hote hain.

Q: Brahmaputra cruise kaisi hoti hai?
A: Luxury river cruise on the Brahmaputra with cabin stay, wildlife watching, bird watching, village visits — 2-4 nights options available.

Q: Northeast expensive hai?
A: Budget mein bhi possible hai — ₹15,000-20,000 per person for a good 5-6 day trip. Infrastructure improve hua hai significantly last few years mein.
