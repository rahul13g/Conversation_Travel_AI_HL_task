# Destination: Lakshadweep (Agatti + Bangaram + Kadmat)

## Overview
Lakshadweep is India's best-kept secret — a pristine archipelago of 36 coral atolls in the Arabian Sea with turquoise lagoons, white sand beaches, and some of the world's richest coral reefs. Largely undeveloped and protected, it offers an experience comparable to the Maldives but within India. Limited tourist capacity makes it exclusive by nature.

## Best For
- Couples and honeymoon
- Scuba diving and snorkeling enthusiasts
- Beach lovers seeking complete seclusion
- Travelers wanting Maldives experience without passport
- Luxury seekers

## Travel Duration
- Short: 4 Nights / 5 Days
- Standard: 5 Nights / 6 Days

## Getting There
- Flights: Only to Agatti Island — from Kochi (45 min) and direct from some cities seasonally
- Ship: From Kochi — 14–20 hours depending on island (not recommended for most)
- Permits: Entry Permit mandatory for all visitors — we handle this

## Best Time to Visit
- Ideal: October to May
- Peak: December to March
- Avoid: June–September (monsoon, rough seas, islands largely inaccessible)

## Important Note
Lakshadweep has limited accommodation and permits. Book well in advance — 3–6 months ahead for peak season.

## Island Highlights

### Agatti Island — Gateway
- Main entry point with the only airport
- Stunning lagoon visible from the plane as you land
- 7.5km long island with beautiful beaches both sides
- Water sports and diving
- Agatti Lagoon — one of the most beautiful lagoons in India

### Bangaram Island — Paradise
- Uninhabited island (only resort guests allowed)
- No locals, no vehicles, no noise — pure seclusion
- Bangaram Island Resort — one of India's most exclusive beach resorts
- Premier diving destination — wrecks, reef, manta rays, sharks
- Absolute privacy — ideal for honeymoon

### Kadmat Island
- 8km long, 500m wide — classic atoll shape
- Excellent scuba diving — coral gardens, sea turtles
- Kadmat Beach — pristine and uncrowded
- Water sports center

### Minicoy Island (Permit required separately)
- Southernmost island, closest to Maldives in character
- Unique Mahl culture (distinct from rest of Lakshadweep)
- Old lighthouse with panoramic views
- Traditional tuna fishing village

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Lakshadweep vs Maldives — kaunsa better hai?
A: Lakshadweep almost same experience — turquoise water, coral reefs, white sand — but no passport needed, INR currency, and often cheaper. Maldives has more resort options and luxury infrastructure. Lakshadweep is more raw and unspoiled.

Q: Entry permit kitne time mein milti hai?
A: 2–3 weeks in advance apply karna chahiye. Hum poora permit process handle karte hain — aapko bas documents dene hote hain.

Q: Lakshadweep mein alcohol milta hai?
A: Lakshadweep Union Territory hai aur largely conservative Muslim population — alcohol restricted on most islands. Bangaram Island Resort serves alcohol as it is an isolated resort island.

Q: Kya non-swimmers ke liye bhi Lakshadweep worth it hai?
A: Haan — shallow lagoons, glass-bottom boats, and beach experiences sab possible hain without swimming. Life jackets available hain for water activities.

Q: Advance booking kitni zaroori hai?
A: Peak season (December–March) mein 4–6 months advance booking strongly recommended. Permits and accommodation both limited hain.
